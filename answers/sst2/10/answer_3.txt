To select the most informative instances for labeling by a human annotator in an active learning scenario, the following steps should be considered:

1. **Diversity in Content**: Choose instances that cover a wide range of topics, styles, or sentiments. This ensures that the labeled data encompasses a broad spectrum of the dataset.

2. **Ambiguity or Difficulty**: Prioritize instances that seem ambiguous or difficult to classify. These instances often provide the most information when labeled because they help to refine the decision boundaries of the model.

3. **Representativeness**: Select instances that are representative of the entire dataset. This helps in building a model that generalizes well.

4. **Balance**: Ensure that there is a balance in terms of the types of instances chosen. For example, if the dataset is about movie reviews, there should be a mix of positive, negative, and neutral reviews.

5. **Uniqueness**: Choose instances that add new information or perspectives not already covered by other instances in the training set.
